Norte Chico (Caral)

South America · 3500 BCE – 1800 BCE

Along the arid coast of Peru, in a handful of river valleys north of modern Lima, the Norte Chico civilization arose as the earliest known complex society in the Americas. Its people, best represented by the site of Caral in the Supe Valley, built monumental platform mounds and sunken circular plazas as early as 3500 BCE — centuries before the first Egyptian pyramids and without any evidence of pottery, writing, or large-scale warfare to accompany them.

Norte Chico's economy rested on an unusual foundation: rather than grain agriculture, communities grew cotton for fishing nets and irrigated crops like squash and beans, trading with coastal fishing villages for the anchoveta and sardines that supplied most of their protein. This cotton-and-seafood economy funded an extraordinary building program. Caral itself covers over 60 hectares and includes six large pyramidal mounds, the largest rising some 18 meters, along with amphitheaters and residential terraces arranged around a planned civic core.

At its height, roughly 3000–1800 BCE, Norte Chico encompassed at least twenty-five major population centers spread across several valleys, apparently linked by shared religious and architectural traditions rather than centralized political control or visible military conquest. Notably absent from the archaeological record are defensive walls, weapons caches, or mutilated remains, suggesting a society organized more around ceremony, trade, and possibly a shared cult than coercive force.

By around 1800 BCE, Caral and its neighboring centers were largely abandoned, possibly due to drought, shifting river courses, or environmental stress from deforestation. The civilization left no writing beyond knotted cord devices called quipus, whose use here is debated, and it remained essentially unknown to archaeology until systematic excavation and radiocarbon dating at Caral in the 1990s revealed its true antiquity. Norte Chico's traditions of platform-mound building and coastal-valley settlement fed into the later cultural sequence of Andean civilization that eventually produced Chavín, Moche, and the Inca.

Meanwhile, elsewhere

  • Africa: Egypt's Old and Middle Kingdoms rose and fell, from the unification of the Nile to the building of the Giza pyramids.
  • Asia: Sumerian city-states developed cuneiform writing, and the Indus Valley civilization built its planned cities at Harappa and Mohenjo-daro.
  • Europe: megalith builders raised Stonehenge, and Minoan palace civilization was taking shape on Crete.
